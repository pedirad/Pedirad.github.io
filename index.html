Research Interests

My primary research interests lie at the intersection of computation, logic, and algorithms within fundamental, molecular, and evolutionary biology, chemistry, and economics. I am particularly interested in addressing foundational problems in the application of chemistry to biological systems through computational logic and algorithmic methods. In addition, I am drawn to applied econometrics and the quantitative analysis of behavioral patterns, especially within the context of behavioral economics.

  1. Developing AI-guided quantum models to analyze charge distribution topologies using QTAIM and EDA during CRISPR-Cas9 DNA cleavage, aiming to minimize unintended off-target mutations.
  2. Using time-resolved quantum simulations to study epigenetic DNA methylation, especially how enzyme internal electric fields act as molecular switches for proton and electron tunneling.
  3. Applying hybrid quantum machine learning to telomeric G-quadruplex structural dynamics, with focus on 3D macromolecular aromaticity, magnetic properties, quantum coherence, and π–π stacking.
  4. Designing bit-based and qubit-based biological logic gates for controlling synthesis pathways, catalysts, and biosensors in vitro and in vivo.
  5. Developing algorithmic optimization frameworks for total synthesis of natural products and pharmaceutical compounds.
  6. Constructing behavioral estimation models in macroeconomics, including predictive and structural modeling of economic decision-making and voting behavior based on game theory.
